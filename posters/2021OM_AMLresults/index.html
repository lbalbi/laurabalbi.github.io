AgreementMakerLight 2013-2021

AgreementMakerLight (AML) is an ontology matching system with a scalable and extensible framework that enabled it to succeed in a variety of ontology matching tasks since it first debuted in the Ontology Alignment Evaluation Initiative (OAEI) back in 2013.

After nine OAEI editions of continued success, we are retiring AML to work on a novel, broader-purpose data integration system.

In this web-poster, we review AML's development and results history.

AML History

AML arose in 2013, within the SOMER project, when, after months of working with AgreementMaker, Daniel realized it would be easier to design a new matching system engine than to make all the changes to AgreementMaker's engine that would be required for it to handle large ontologies efficiently. SOMER coordinator, Francisco, gave Daniel one month to develop a first pilot and prove his point, and one month later AML was born.

Drawing from the knowledge accumulated in AgreementMaker, AML added scalability to the design principles of flexibility and extensibility, while augmenting the lexical weighting and background knowledge components that Catia had already contributed to AgreementMaker. Emanuel, another core member of the SOMER project, was given the challenge of developing a logic repair algorithm for AML, which he also managed in record time. Isabel, SOMER's external advisor and AgreementMaker's coordinator, provided invaluable insight that helped steer the development of AML.

Throughout the years, we kept working on improving and extending AML, with many collaborators contributing to its development.

For AML's latest developments, please check our web-poster detailing AMLC's association rule mining strategy for complex ontology alignment.

OAEI results

In 2013, AML actively participated in three tracks--Anatomy, Conference, and Large Biomedical Ontologies--though it was also evaluated in Benchmark, Library and Multifarm.

Since then, we have gradually expanded AML capabilities, and the number of tracks it could tackle. Over the last nine years, counting instance-matching sub-tracks, AML actively participated in 18 distinct OAEI tracks.

You can check out AML's performance in the latest OAEI 2021 Results.
